Acne Diary

This was when I first started taking antibiotics as a quick option for a painful, uncontrollable breakout caused by mask use during the COVID-19 pandemic. This was also when I began to understand the importance of proper ventilation.

Very quickly, the antibiotics significantly improved my skin, but it’s well known that they should be avoided whenever possible.

With consistent moisturizing, spot treatment creams, a clean pillowcase, and keeping my hands off my face, my skin slowly began to recover.

The scarring started to heal, but acne kept returning, which made me rely heavily on benzoyl peroxide creams.

Even as an adult, acne would still appear, so I decided to spend $100 on a private dermatologist, who finally prescribed isotretinoin.

One year after treatment, along with consistent cleansing and moisturizing, my skin has improved significantly. Acne rarely returns, even during stressful periods, though some hyperpigmentation remains. I was advised to get laser treatment — which I didn’t as it is quite expensive.
